Our Foundation

About PCGT

The Public Concern for Governance Trust (hereinafter "the Trust" or "PCGT") was formally established in the year 2002 by its founding trustees: Mr. Julio Ribeiro, Dr. Ram Kumar Anand, and the late Mr. Bhalchandra Gopal Deshmukh. The Trust was constituted as a principled civil society organisation, conceived with the overarching objective of strengthening civic engagement and public activism in the service of transparent, accountable, and effective governance within the framework of India's constitutional order.

The vision of PCGT is firmly anchored in the pursuit of exemplary governance, the systemic mitigation of corruption, the cultivation of ethical values in public life, and the promotion of harmonious relations among the diverse communities of India—a principle broadly characterised under the concept of Sadbhavana. The Trust recognises that the endeavour to combat corruption entails profound structural and societal challenges. Accordingly, PCGT has consistently adopted a values-driven and participatory approach to institutional reform, one that prioritises moral reasoning and civic responsibility alongside legal and administrative intervention.

By fostering ethical consciousness, civic responsibility, and the capacity for moral reasoning among students, the Trust seeks to nurture a generation of informed and conscientious citizens capable of contributing meaningfully to the governance of the nation. The Trust reflects a shared commitment to the advancement of transparent and accountable governance in the Republic of India.

Our Institution

About PGCL & SVKM

Shri Vile Parle Kelavani Mandal (SVKM) is a Public Charitable Trust registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860 and The Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950, whose educational legacy dates back to 1934, when it adopted Rashtriya Shala—a school founded in 1921 in the spirit of India's National Movement and whose ethos is firmly rooted in patriotic fervour, selfless service, and the spirit of indigenous enterprise that emerged during the freedom struggle; over the decades, the Mandal has evolved into a leading educational conglomerate imparting quality education across disciplines to more than 32,000 students.

Established in 2004 under the aegis of SVKM, Pravin Gandhi College of Law (PGCL) was conceived to impart rigorous, contemporary, and socially responsive legal education aligned with the evolving demands of a globalised legal profession, and has been accorded linguistic minority status for the Gujarati community.

PGCL follows a holistic, student-centric pedagogical model that seeks to identify, nurture, and refine the inherent creativity and intellectual potential of every learner through innovative teaching methodologies, experiential learning, moot court and trial advocacy, debates, seminars, workshops, interdisciplinary engagement, and student-led initiatives such as L'Avocat and the Juris-Cine Club, while consciously situating the study of law within its broader social, political, economic, and ethical contexts.

The College is an A++ Accredited institution, having been awarded an 'A+' Grade with a CGPA of 3.28 by the National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC), is approved by the Bar Council of India (BCI), is permanently affiliated to the University of Mumbai, and is duly recognised under the All India Survey on Higher Education (AISHE) with Code C-33901, thereby reaffirming its academic standing, regulatory compliance, and institutional credibility; supported by an accomplished faculty, a well-resourced library, and robust co-curricular and sports programmes, PGCL aspires to be an institution of excellence in legal education, producing socially sensitive, environmentally conscious, and professionally competent graduates committed to upholding the rule of law with integrity, compassion, and a deep sense of social responsibility.

The Initiative

Model Youth Parliament

The PCGT's Model Youth Parliament (hereinafter referred to as "MYP"), constituted and administered by the Public Concern for Governance Trust (PCGT), is an experiential educational initiative designed to simulate the functioning of a parliamentary legislative system with the objective of cultivating informed democratic competence among youth participants. The Programme operates on the pedagogical premise that constitutional literacy and governance awareness are most effectively internalised through structured simulation and participatory immersion, and accordingly recreates parliamentary processes including debates, motions, committee scrutiny, and mechanisms of executive accountability.

Through the participation of over four hundred students drawn from across India, the Programme reflects a national and pluralistic character while fostering constitutional socialisation, analytical reasoning, policy research capability, and evidence-based decision making. Proceedings are conducted under the supervision of an experienced Executive Board to ensure procedural authenticity and institutional discipline consistent with recognised parliamentary conventions. Participants function in capacities analogous to Members of Parliament, with designated student leaders undertaking enhanced responsibilities in maintaining decorum, facilitating consensus, and advancing deliberative outcomes.

The Programme further promotes democratic temperament, tolerance for plural viewpoints, and professional orientation by facilitating interaction with eminent jurists and civil servants and by providing merit-based incentives, including cash awards and internship opportunities with PCGT. Accordingly, the MYP constitutes a structured nation-building platform integrating constitutional theory with simulated legislative practice and is directed towards nurturing procedurally informed, governance-oriented youth committed to transparency, accountability, and reasoned democratic engagement.

Theme 2026

Samvidhan · Sadbhavana · Samarpan

"Good governance is not merely about institutions and laws; it is about the moral capacity of citizens to make democracy work."
— Justice Rajendra Mal Lodha, Former Chief Justice of Supreme Court of India

The theme "Samvidhan, Sadbhavana, Samarpan: Youth Commitment to Good Governance" integrates India's civilisational wisdom with contemporary democratic needs. Samvidhan, derived from Sanskrit, signifies a collectively accepted normative order. In modern constitutionalism, it embodies the rule of law, separation of powers, and fundamental rights. Dr. B. R. Ambedkar cautioned that even the finest Constitution depends upon constitutional morality for its success.

Sadbhavana represents an ethical disposition of goodwill, harmony, and empathy. It resonates with the Preamble's promise of fraternity and is supported by modern behavioural science, which confirms that trust and cooperation strengthen institutional effectiveness. In a plural society, law alone cannot sustain order without internalised social respect.

Samarpan, meaning complete dedication, reflects the spirit of karma yoga and finds constitutional expression in the Fundamental Duties under Article 51A. Governance reforms succeed only when driven by committed citizens.

Together, these principles position youth as active custodians of democracy. When young citizens understand the Constitution, practise ethical harmony, and dedicate themselves to public service, good governance transforms from theory into lived reality, ensuring a resilient and morally grounded Republic.

For as Bharat Ratna Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam, Former President of the Republic of India, wisely affirmed, a nation's strength ultimately resides not in its laws alone but in the character and conscience of its young citizens, and it is through such conscious commitment that good governance shall endure, evolve, and be justly secured for generations yet to come.
